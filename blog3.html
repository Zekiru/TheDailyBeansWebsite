What the World Wants

A Blog Post by Ryellie Estandarte, May 27 2021.

The SGD(Sustainable Development Goals) is what the world wants. The purpose of this is to produce a set of universal goals that would help combat the urgent environmental, political and economic challenges facing our world.

The SDG has a collection of 17 interlinked global goals for again the better and more sustainable future for all. It was set up in 2015 by the United Nations General Assembly and are intended to be achieved by the year 2030.

So how can the youth contribute to achieving the SDGs? For one see to it what SDG your country needs to focus at, what SDG your country needs. That's a good start on trying to contribute though like all things it is easier said than done. Start small and simple the youth still has time to grow there's no need to rush on your plan on making things better.

You can try donating to charity and giving food to the less fortunate. Raising awareness on the problems of the world can also help and even participating in activities that can help both bodies of land and water generally helping out nature.

Now this might not seem like a lot but as Jose Rizal said "The youth is the hope of our future." We can still improve over time. One day will be the ones making the big decisions for this world so the little things we do in the past will help in the long run.
