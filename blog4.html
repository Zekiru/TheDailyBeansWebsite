Gender Equality

A Blog Post by Krystiane Yrwin Durana, May 21 2021.

Gender Equality is not just a women’s issue, it is the government, society, and human rights issue that must need to take part and take action. Why do we need gender roles when there are lots of people who are more than masculine and feminine? Why is there a ‘man’ in every ‘woman’, a ‘he’ in every ‘she’, a ‘male’ in every ‘female’? Because it means that a woman can do things a man can do. A man’s job can be a woman’s job too. A woman’s responsibilities can be a man’s responsibilities too.

Stop gender stereotyping, we are all loved and created equally by God, which is why we should treat every person and every gender fairly as a human and with the same respect. Give them their rights to live freely No one is superior or better or more intelligent or more hard-working because at the end of the day (or should I say, at the end of our very last breath), we will be buried in a grave where ALL genders are equally buried. Everyone is unique in their own special ways. Everyone isn’t perfect, we have flaws and insecurities, but it doesn’t mean that we should discriminate them.

Discrimination (and/or Inequality) and Violence is a very serious topic to talk about. But what are the common reasons why people discriminate against others? It’s mostly because of their sex, gender, race, status, religion, and even their age. According to some researches and from my perspective, people discriminate because they are insecure.

They think that others are better and superior to their selves. “DO NOT do unto others what you don’t want them to do unto you” is a golden rule from Matthew 7:12 or Confucius and I love this rule because I know that karma exists and it helps me prevent doing any actions that will lead me or other people to any harm.

As a woman, I am completely against different forms of violence and discrimination that women and all genders experience. In this opportunity to change, we are talking about how to make the world peaceful, healthy, and safe. The road to gender equity begins with motivating, educating, and empowering young people’s minds in taking action towards these kinds of situations in their communities.

I believe that the youth can give to achieving the SDG 5-Gender Equality by joining and supporting campaigns, organizations, foundations, and events about women empowerment, gender inequalities, domestic abuses, discriminations, and other programs about human rights. I believe that the youth can really make in contributing because most youths are part of the LGBTQ+ community and because of that, I am sure that they want equality and freedom as well.

Women and LGBTQ+ communities should develop more knowledge, skills, and confidence in order for the campaigns and organizations to be effective. They should not let the men be dominant or the “alpha” in their environment.

So why does Equality important not only in our society but also in the world? Because having and achieving equality brings peace and confidence to everyone around the world and so that no one will be left behind.
