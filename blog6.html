Youth Creating Social Awareness

A Blog Post by Sophia Janelle Parada, May 20 2021.

With determination, perseverance, and thorough understanding, the youth can actually be huge driving forces in achieving the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). The 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development calls on member countries to exert their best effort to achieve the goals that they set over the next fifteen (15) years.

Such goals aim to curb extreme poverty and starvation, achieve universal primary education, promote gender equality, empower women, prevent pollution, reduce greenhouse gases, and conserve resources through reusing and recycling.

The youth can create social awareness and play a crucial role in the implementation and monitoring of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Being innovators, they can bring fresh perspectives, offer bright ideas and alternative solutions to address pressing concerns. Through social media platforms, they can talk about hard-hitting issues regarding gender equality, race and transgender rights, and raise their voices against discrimination.

They can demand action on climate change and urge the government to take global warming seriously. As an individual, the youth can use less energy-intensive transport such as shared rides, bikes, and public means of conveyance. When they have to go to places that are near, they can just walk instead. They can also avoid food wastage by buying only those that they can consume. Meals for the entire week must be planned and a list of the necessary grocery items should be prepared to avoid overbuying and minimize spoilage.

As future leaders, it is imperative that the youth assume pivotal role in the successful implementation of the SDGs that will benefit the world at large. These may be simple things, but these simple things go a long way, and these simple things are what matter the most.

As early as now, we must quit the mindset that there is someone out there other than ourselves who will save our environment and everything in it. If we continue this cycle without doing anything ourselves, we are not helping resolve the problems that confront us.e must keep in mind that nothing good comes out of nothing, that is why we should do something.
